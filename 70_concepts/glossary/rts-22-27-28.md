# RTS 22 / 27 / 28 — MiFID II Reporting Standards

Three numbered MiFID II Regulatory Technical Standards that an EMS frequently touches:

- **RTS 22 — Transaction Reporting**. Investment firms must report **every transaction in a financial instrument** to the regulator (via an ARM — Approved Reporting Mechanism — or directly to ESMA/national-competent-authority) **by T+1**. 65 fields per transaction. The EU counterpart to FINRA CAT, but with different field definitions and a per-firm rather than per-venue reporting model.
- **RTS 27 — Execution Quality Reports**. **Execution venues** (regulated markets, MTFs, OTFs, SIs) must publish **quarterly execution-quality reports** with price, cost, speed, and likelihood-of-execution statistics per instrument. Discontinued in 2021 under MiFID II "quick fix" but historically important — broker note examples reference it.
- **RTS 28 — Top 5 Venues Report**. **Investment firms** must publish an **annual report listing their top 5 execution venues** (by class of financial instrument) and a summary of execution quality. This survives the "quick fix"; firms must produce it yearly.

Together they constitute the MiFID II best-execution evidence framework. RTS 22 is the daily / per-trade operational burden; RTS 28 is the annual public disclosure.

## Example

A UK-MiFID firm executes 500 trades a day across EU equities, FI, and FX. Daily: 500 RTS 22 reports flow to its ARM and onward to FCA / ESMA. Annually: it publishes an RTS 28 report listing (per asset class) the top 5 venues used and high-level execution-quality observations.
