# MEMX (Members Exchange)

**Members Exchange** is a US national securities exchange **founded and owned by a consortium of broker-dealers and market makers** (BAML, Charles Schwab, Citadel Securities, E*TRADE, Fidelity, GTS, JPM, Morgan Stanley, TD Ameritrade, UBS, Virtu) to provide a lower-cost alternative to NYSE/Nasdaq.

## Asset classes

- US-listed equities (all NMS securities)
- Equity options (MEMX Options, separate venue)

## Workflow mechanisms

- **CLOB** with price-time priority.
- **Lower fees** than NYSE/Nasdaq by design (member-owned model).
- Simplified order types compared to legacy exchanges.

## Connectivity

- **MEMOIR (binary)** — proprietary low-latency protocol.
- **FIX 4.2 / 4.4** for institutional access.
- Market data via MEMX feed.

## Key facts

- Founded 2019, launched 2020 — youngest of the major US equity venues.
- Member-ownership creates direct alignment between routing brokers and venue.
- Together with [[iex]], represents the post-2010 wave of US exchange entrants.
